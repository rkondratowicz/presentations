## Style Name: "Editorial / Magazine Cover"

**Overall feel:** Quiet, print-like, essayistic. Reads like a feature spread from a design-conscious tech magazine, not a slide template. Structure (rules, kicker, issue number) does the visual work instead of color or decoration.

### Colors
- Background: `#f5f2ec` — warm off-white/cream, not pure white
- Primary text/ink: `#17140f` — near-black, warm-toned
- Secondary/muted text: `#6b6455` (kicker italics, issue line) and `#3a352c` (body/subhead)
- Accent (used sparingly, one place only): `#b23b1e` — a brick/rust red, applied only to italicized emphasis words in the headline

### Typography
- **Display/headline:** , "Iowan Old Style" falling back to serif. Large size (~76px at 1280×720), regular weight (not bold), tight negative letter-spacing (-0.01em), line-height ~1.0. Emphasis words set in italic and in the accent color.
- **Kicker/eyebrow:** sans-serif (system default), small (~14px), uppercase, letter-spacing 0.14em, bold (700)
- **Issue line / byline:** Georgia italic, small (~15px), muted color
- **Subhead/body:** sans-serif, ~19px, regular weight, muted ink color, line-height ~1.5, constrained max-width (don't let it span the full slide)
- **Footer numbering:** Georgia, ~13px, muted, right-aligned

### Layout structure
- Full-bleed slide, generous padding (~6–7% top/bottom, 7% sides)
- **Top band:** kicker (left) + issue/byline (right), baseline-aligned, sitting on a solid 2px bottom rule
- **Middle band:** the headline, vertically centered in the remaining space, left-aligned
- **Bottom band:** subhead (left) + a small numbered/tag line (right), separated from the headline area by a thin 1px top rule

### Signature elements (what makes it recognizable)
- The **two horizontal rules** (thick above content, thin below) that bracket the slide like a masthead — this is the one structural device to keep consistent across every slide in the deck, not just the title slide.
- **Selective italic + accent color** on exactly one word or phrase per headline — never more than one emphasis per slide.
- Treating each slide like a "page" with an issue number / section number in the corner, as if the deck were a print publication with a running numbering system.

### What to avoid (keeps it from drifting generic)
- No gradients, no drop shadows, no rounded corners with heavy radius
- No more than one accent color total
- Don't bold the headline — regular-weight serif at large size is what gives it the editorial calm; bolding pushes it back toward "startup pitch deck"
- Keep body copy narrow (don't let subheads run full-width)
